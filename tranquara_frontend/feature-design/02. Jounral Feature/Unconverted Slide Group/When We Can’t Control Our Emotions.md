
## 🧩 **SlideGroup Title:**

### *When We Can’t Control Our Emotions*

**Goal:**
Help users understand why emotional control sometimes fails, how “scripts” form, and how to weaken the link between triggers and emotional reactions.

---

### 🧠 **Slide 1 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** Why Can’t We Control Some Emotions?
**Content:**
We lose control when a trigger matches an **evolved emotional theme** (deeply rooted in survival) or a **learned trigger** that closely resembles it.
In these moments, emotional reflexes override logic — like an automatic program firing before we can think.

---

### ⚡ **Slide 2 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** The Refractory State
**Content:**
When an emotion is activated, we enter a *refractory state* — a temporary period when our thinking **cannot process information** that contradicts or challenges that emotion.
During this time, we often justify or maintain the feeling rather than re-evaluate it.

💡 *Example:* During anger, even calm reasoning from others might feel invalid or irritating.

---

### 💭 **Slide 3 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** Emotional “Scripts” — The Loops That Keep Us Stuck
**Content:**
Unresolved feelings create “scripts”: repeating emotional patterns that distort reality.
They form when:

* Emotions were never fully expressed or resolved.
* Expression didn’t lead to a desired outcome.

Scripts make new situations *feel like old wounds*, causing inappropriate reactions or prolonged emotional episodes.

---

### 🧩 **Slide 4 — Prompt Slide**

**Title:** Identify Your Emotional Script
**Prompt:**
“Is there a recurring emotional pattern in your life — something that always triggers a similar reaction? What old feeling might that be connected to?”

---

### 🪞 **Slide 5 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** Should We Erase Emotions?
**Content:**
We don’t want to *remove* emotions — they’re not like an appendix we can discard.
Emotions make life meaningful.
What we seek is the **ability to regulate specific emotional triggers**, not suppress emotion altogether.

🪶 *Key takeaway:* Emotions are vital; control comes from understanding, not elimination.

---

### 🔗 **Slide 6 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** How to Weaken Emotional Triggers
**Content:**
You can’t delete a trigger — but you can **break the connection** between the trigger and the emotional reaction.

* The *cell assembly* (memory pattern) still exists.
* You’re retraining your brain not to activate it automatically.

🧩 *Analogy:* Like removing a record’s relationship in a database — the record remains, but it no longer drives behavior.

However, under **stress or fatigue**, the old connection can resurface — so self-care and awareness are key.

---

### 💬 **Slide 7 — Prompt Slide**

**Title:** Weakening a Trigger in Your Life
**Prompt:**
“Think of a situation that often causes you stress or anger. What could help you pause before reacting next time?”

---

### ⚖️ **Slide 8 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** 6 Factors That Affect Emotional Trigger Strength
**Content:**
1️⃣ **Closeness to evolved theme:** primal triggers (e.g., danger, betrayal) are stronger.
2️⃣ **Resemblance to original event:** similar situations reactivate old patterns.
3️⃣ **Age of learning:** earlier triggers are harder to unlearn.
4️⃣ **Initial emotional charge:** the more intense the original emotion, the stronger the link.
5️⃣ **Affective style:** some people feel and recover faster than others.
6️⃣ **Stress & fatigue:** lower self-regulation makes old links reappear.

---

### 🧘 **Slide 9 — Doc Slide**

**Title:** Moods vs Emotions
**Content:**

* **Emotions** are quick — seconds or minutes.
* **Moods** linger — hours or days.
  Moods shape how we interpret events:
  When irritable, we *seek* reasons to be angry; minor events feel exaggerated.

💡 *Analogy:* Think of emotions as **sparks**, and moods as **the fuel** that keeps them burning.

---

### 🪞 **Slide 10 — Reflection Slide**

**Title:** Your Mood–Emotion Link
**Prompt:**
“How does your mood influence the emotions you feel most often? Can you think of a time when a bad mood made everything feel worse?”

---

### 🌿 **Slide 11 — Summary Slide**

**Title:** Key Takeaways
**Content:**

* Emotions can override logic when deeply rooted or triggered by old patterns.
* The *refractory state* blinds us to new perspectives.
* Emotional “scripts” replay unresolved feelings.
* We can weaken triggers, but not erase them — and awareness is the first step.
* Managing moods helps us manage emotions.
